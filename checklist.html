New Adopter Checklist

Preparing for your new furry family member is exciting! Use this checklist to make sure you have everything ready for their arrival.

Before Your Pet Arrives:

  • Food & Water Bowls: Get sturdy, easy-to-clean bowls.
  • Appropriate Food: Ask the shelter or previous owner what food your pet is currently eating to ensure a smooth transition.
  • Collar & ID Tag: Essential for identification. Include your contact information.
  • Leash & Harness (for dogs): Choose a comfortable and secure option.
  • Bed/Crate: A comfortable, safe space for your pet to sleep and relax.
  • Toys: A variety of toys to keep them entertained and mentally stimulated.
  • Grooming Supplies: Brush, nail clippers, pet-friendly shampoo.
  • Litter Box & Litter (for cats): Place in a quiet, accessible location.
  • Scratching Post (for cats): To protect your furniture.
  • Pet-Proof Your Home: Remove any hazards, secure cabinets, and store toxic substances out of reach.
  • Veterinarian Appointment: Schedule a check-up within the first few days of adoption.
  • Research Pet Insurance: Consider options for unexpected vet bills.

First Few Days/Weeks:

  • Establish a Routine: Consistent feeding, potty breaks, and playtime.
  • Introduce Gradually: If you have other pets, introduce them slowly and under supervision.
  • Positive Reinforcement: Reward good behavior with treats, praise, and affection.
  • Socialization: Gradually expose your pet to new people, places, and experiences.
  • Training: Start basic obedience training early (for dogs) or reinforce good habits (for cats).
  • Patience is Key: Your new pet needs time to adjust to their new environment and family.

Remember, every pet is unique. Observe your new companion and adjust their care to meet their individual needs.
